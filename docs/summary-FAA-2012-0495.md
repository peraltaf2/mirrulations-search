## Comments Summary

The docket received **1 public comment** during the comment period. The comment addressed brake compliance issues related to the proposed directive.

### Comment 1: FAA-2012-0495-0003
**Gulfstream Aerospace Comment on Brake Service Compliance**

- **Comment ID**: FAA-2012-0495-0003
- **Organization**: Gulfstream Aerospace
- **Submitter**: Gene Herrera (Self-represented)
- **Location**: Dallas, TX 75071, United States
- **Posted Date**: July 17, 2012
- **Received Date**: July 17, 2012
- **Tracking Number**: 8109de63
- **Government Agency Type**: Federal (FAA)
- **Status**: Not withdrawn, not open for comment
- **Document Modified**: August 30, 2013

### Comment Details

**Subject**: Reference to Docket No. FAA-2012-0495; Directorate Identifier 2011-NM-236-AD

**Summary**: Gulfstream Aerospace submitted a detailed comment regarding brake service compliance on Gulfstream 200 aircraft. Key points include:

1. **Service Bulletin Issued**: On October 20, 2011, Gulfstream issued service bulletin 200-32-389 to address degraded brake performance
2. **Issue Discovery**: Investigation revealed the root cause was brake wear indicator pins that had been cut longer than specified in the manufacturer's Component Maintenance Manual (CMM) during overhaul at Gulfstream's in-house Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM) approved brake Repair and Overhaul (R&O) facility
3. **Fleet Compliance**: As of July 16, 2012, Gulfstream reported 98% fleet compliance with either the original issue or revision 1 of the service bulletin
4. **Non-compliant Aircraft**: Three aircraft remained non-compliant as of June 25, 2012:
   - G200 S/N 012: Totaled and out of service
   - G200 S/N 022: Out of service, scheduled for pre-buy inspection and service bulletin compliance
   - G200 S/N 035: Scheduled for heavy maintenance on July 17, 2012 for service bulletin compliance and calendar maintenance

### Comment Significance

This comment represents manufacturer response to the proposed airworthiness directive, providing fleet compliance data and demonstrating corrective actions taken through service bulletins rather than requiring an AD.
